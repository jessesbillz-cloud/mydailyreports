Here's What Really Happens

7 AM: Walking around site doing your counts, seeing who's onsite and what's going on for the day. Down in a footing looking at rebar and then off to an OAC meeting.

10 AM: Now how many concrete guys were there again? Off to lunch and another site quick to look at some solar panel bolting, taking voice notes if needed and a few photos.

Afternoon: Back to the main job for some underslab electrical and helping the GC out with a few things, discussing some details. Sit down to read the RFIs for the day and check your email to find 2 inspection requests dropped in there at 10am.

3:30 PM: You get off before sitting in an hour of traffic home. 2 hours of daylight before you retreat in the house to start transcribing your day from a mess of handwritten notes, pictures and 6 minutes of voice notes for 2 different jobs.

Evening: Did you review that submittal on the electrical splices against the spec and district standards from the solar project yet? So another hour of typing, copying, pasting, sending photos, moving and resizing them. Then you rename, create an email, PDF the document and upload it. Send it off and you get to do it all again tomorrow.

I've got better things to do with my time.

I wanted to produce a better product on a daily basis than I had been, and I can do it while I walk the site.
